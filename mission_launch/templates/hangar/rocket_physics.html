⚙️ Rocket Physics

Explore the physics that makes rocketry fly — from the forces at liftoff to your rocket’s soaring altitude.

🚀 Key Rocket Physics Topics:

  • Altitude Estimation: Calculate how high your rocket might fly based on thrust, weight, and drag.
  • Weight (Mass): The total mass of your rocket affects acceleration and maximum height.
  • Drag: Air resistance slows your rocket. Shape and surface finish matter.
  • Lift-Off Force: The engine’s thrust must exceed your rocket’s weight to leave the pad.

Formulas & Explanations:

🌌 Lift-Off Thrust vs Weight

Force Required to Lift-Off:
\( F_{thrust} > m \times g \)

Where:
\( F_{thrust} \) = Engine thrust (Newtons)
\( m \) = Mass of rocket (kg)
\( g \) = Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s²)

🛫 Altitude Estimation (Simplified)

For a basic estimate ignoring drag:
\( h = \frac{(F_{thrust} - m \times g) \times t_{burn}}{2m} \)

Where:
\( h \) = Height (meters)
\( t_{burn} \) = Engine burn time (seconds)

🌬️ Drag Force

Drag slows your rocket’s ascent:
\( F_{drag} = \frac{1}{2} \times \rho \times v^2 \times C_d \times A \)

Where:
\( \rho \) = Air density (kg/m³)
\( v \) = Velocity (m/s)
\( C_d \) = Drag coefficient
\( A \) = Cross-sectional area (m²)

🪶 Rocket Weight

Total rocket mass includes:

  • Body tube & fins
  • Engine weight
  • Electronics
  • Recovery system (e.g. parachute)
Knowing this mass helps choose the right engine.
