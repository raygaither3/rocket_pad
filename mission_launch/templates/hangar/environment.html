🌦️ Environment Variables

Understand how humidity, altitude, and wind affect rocket performance. Planning for conditions ensures safer flights and better altitude predictions.

Key Environmental Factors

  • Humidity: Moist air is less dense, potentially improving altitude slightly but increasing motor ignition delays.
  • Altitude: Higher launch sites have lower air pressure, reducing drag and boosting altitude potential.
  • Wind: Strong winds can carry rockets off-course and require larger recovery areas.
  • Temperature: Affects air density and engine performance.

Did You Know?

Launching at high altitudes can add significant meters to your rocket’s flight—but may also require tweaks to chute sizing due to lower air resistance.
