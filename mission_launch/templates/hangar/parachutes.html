🪂 Parachute Systems

Match chute size to rocket weight for safe landings every time. A properly sized recovery system ensures your rocket returns safely without damage.

Why Chute Sizing Matters

  • Descent Rate: Controls how quickly your rocket comes down.
  • Rocket Weight: Heavier rockets need larger chutes.
  • Wind Conditions: Affects drift distance during descent.

Basic Chute Sizing Formula

Chute diameter can be roughly estimated by:
\( D = \sqrt{\frac{(2 \times W)}{\rho \times C_d \times V^2}} \)

Where:
\( D \) = Chute diameter (meters)
\( W \) = Rocket weight (Newtons)
\( \rho \) = Air density (kg/m³)
\( C_d \) = Drag coefficient of chute
\( V \) = Desired descent velocity (m/s)
